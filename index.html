YAM & KONTOMIRE RECIPE!!!

What is it?

Kontomire stew also known as palava sauce is a Ghanaian delicacy made with cocoyam leaves popularly called Kontomire. It goes very well with boiled yam, plantain, rice or cocoyam. Kontomire stew is mostly made with smoked salom...my favourite, and melon seeds (agushie) But i mostely enjoy it with the Yam because it goes very well with it and sweet too.

What you need.

Ingredients.

  • 1 cup Palm Oil or Vegetable Oil.
  • 1 maggie cube.
  • Fish or Meat.
  • Pepper.
  • Agushie.
  • Garlic.
  • Chopped Kontomire.
  • 2 Chopped Onion.
  • 3 large diced tomato.
  • Yam.

Ready to Prepare.

Directions.

  1. Heat the oil in a medium pan and fry the onions and garlic until golden. Add pepper and the tomatoes.
  2. Cook for 10 to 15 minutes on low heat, stiring regularly but not continuously.
  3. Wash kontomire and cook until it becomes soft. 🍀
  4. Wash the fish or meat, remove all bones and break into pieces and add to the stew.
  5. Pour agushie in a bowl, add salt and stir. Add the maggie cube and mixture to the stew. 🧄
  6. Drain any water from thawed out kontomire and add it to the stew. Cover and simmer on low heat for 10 to 15 minutes.
  7. Stire regularly, take care not to break up the fish in the stew too much.
  8. Alow to simmer under low heat while you peel the yam.
  9. Cut into cubes, wash twice and boil with enough water. Add salt. 🧂
  10. When the yam is soft, take it out on fire and pour the water out and transfer it into plate. 😋
  11. Ready to serve your Yam and Kontomire stew. 🍲
